An aside about this last thing.

I had a conversation with Michele Flynn about this which posted yesterday, and her post on the topic and our conversation seems to be causing quite a semantic debate on what counts as “review and approve” means, and how to fix what looks to be like $60M+ oversight going back over two decades.

Feedback sent to her from a Metro Council Member:

They have sent their budget breakdown to the Finance Department every year, and that information has been available if a council member or community member wanted to ask for it . Now that the breakdown in actually reporting to council has been pointed out, that has been corrected, and we will be on the look-out for a budget detail by April 15 of every year.

The online convo about it has also been kinda inflammatory, with Jelly Roll songs and Fake News gifs counting as public discourse.

I never want to be confused as a journalist.1 I went to school for the Western Classics (which is why I am qualified to speak with authority all things involving white supremacy and colonialism. jk. sorta). Since this is definitely above my pay grade, I’ve gone ahead and looped in who might be the right people to look into this issue.

The TN Comptroller may or may not have jurisdiction on what happens with Sales Tax and special assessment funds in a Business Improvement District, but it seems like who they’re supposed to flag if you’ve got questions — so hopefully this all sorts itself out soon and we can move forward.

Anyway, I’ve been involved with a lot of high profile things (if you consider local politics high profile). Much of it has been turned around on short notice and little sleep.

There’s been more collaboration than ever before with this content, but also a lot just on my shoulders. That last part is on me — some people have been thanking me for this work, but notably no one is demanding it of me.

Some quotes have been ringing in my head of late. There’s an intense tension between two voices inside me, and it’s showing up in my personal life in ways that aren’t sustainable.

One side shouts:

  • Journalism is printing what someone else does not want printed; everything else is public relations!

  • Never doubt that a small group of thoughtful, committed citizens can change the world; indeed, it's the only thing that ever has!

  • Comfort the afflicted! Afflict the comfortable!

The other side whispers:

  • We must believe we are worthy of rest. We don’t have to earn it. It is our birth‑right.

  • Caring for myself is not self‑indulgence, it is self‑preservation, and that is an act of political warfare.

  • It takes courage to say yes to rest and play in a culture where exhaustion is seen as a status symbol.

I’m taking a little break to listen to the whispers better, and hopefully I can comeback with more of a both/and headspace. I don’t know what that integrated voice sounds like right now.

I was told an adage recently that’s said in high end restaurants: “Slow is smooth. Smooth is fast.”

I need this to all be smoother. Fewer typos. Fewer last minute things rushed out. More intentional collaboration. More experiments in storytelling with testing before release.

I’m aiming for two weeks off from all this.

I think I just heard a deep collective sigh of relief from our city’s bureaucrats and business class.

I need some time to figure out what I want from this platform that we’ve built together. If you’ve valued this work, feel free to consider ask that of yourself: what would you want from this combination megaphone and movement journalism outlet we’ve built together?

Feel free to email me at michaeltlacy@gmail.com with your ideas.

So I’m gonna be off the grid this weekend, and going to be pressing pause on posting for a couple of weeks.

But I’m going to come back with some new ideas and projects we can do together. Probably less “breaking news” and more “collaboratively redesigning systems.”
