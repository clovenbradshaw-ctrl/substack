Last Friday I reported on some ongoing issues at Strobel House, Nashville’s first-of-its-kind permanent supportive housing facility. On Thursday, Strobel House celebrated its first year of operations. This first year, as I’ve been updating readers, has been rocky.

Along with an unreported near-fatal violent incident (which Metro’s Office of Homeless Service’s senior officials were aware of but do not seem to have notified anyone else), an unreported fire that has caused extensive damage to the second floor of the facility, it’s also been uncovered that the vendor, Depaul USA, was operating without a contract for 10 months (while somehow receiving compensation from Metro).

In total, Depaul received over $500k for services for which they did not have a valid contract.

Despite these challenges, Depaul’s Executive Director Chuck Levesque spoke at Thursday’s anniversary for Strobel House, enthusiastic about a continuing partnership with Metro Nashville, which had just inked a deal for $3.5M to get them back in a contract.

On Monday Depaul posted a job listing for his position.

Reports are that Levesque, who had been ED for 15 years, will be stepping down by the end of next week.

If you know any qualified Executive Director-types in the Chicago Area, Depaul is urgently hiring.

Depaul USA seeks a visionary leader to diversify revenue and unite program cities under a shared mission to end homelessness with dignity. Reporting to the Board of Directors of Depaul USA with a dotted line to the Group Chief Executive of Depaul International, the CEO will be the strategic and operational leader of Depaul USA. The CEO will embody Vincentian values, fostering a high-performing and inclusive workplace. As part of Depaul International which operates in seven countries, the CEO will have the opportunity to collaborate with and leverage the global network.

Depaul USA is a dynamic, faith-inspired national nonprofit on a mission to end homelessness and change lives. Founded in 2009 as part of the global Depaul International Group, the organization launched its first program in Philadelphia serving 60 men. Today, from its Chicago headquarters, Depaul USA operates in 12 cities, runs 27 programs, and impacts more than 6,800 individuals each year. The organization is governed by a dedicated 14-member Board of Directors, has a combined capital and operating budget of $11.6M, 140 (70 FTEs) and a corps of volunteers.

Rooted in the Vincentian values of compassion, dignity, and practical action, Depaul USA offers a continuum of care—from supportive and affordable housing to day programs, medical respite, and social enterprise. In 2024, Depaul USA served over 99,444 meals, helped 124 people secure employment, and moved 208 individuals into permanent housing. Every program is designed to break the cycle of homelessness by improving health, economic stability, and long-term housing security.
