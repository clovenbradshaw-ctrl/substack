Stunning images have been circulating online, and appear to have been obtained though not formally published yet by The Nashville Scene.

They include never before seen views from inside of the downtown library parking garage after it suffered an explosion whose cause is as yet “undetermined” according to the Nashville Fire Department.

The cement and steel structure, managed by the non-profit The Downtown Partnership, which is largely funded through funds collected by Metro Nashville government, mysteriously exploded in the early hours of June 10.

The most that has been said on the matter is The Downtown Partnership has pointed at its subcontractor, Block by Block. Block by Block performs the maintenance and landscaping (and possibly also the street outreach) duties The Downtown Partnership is contracted to implement.

The images are stunning. They bring to mind scenes from the Chernobyl disaster.

Civilian cars, presumably of guests at the adjoining Renaissance Hotel, are burnt out shells.

What appear to be the remains of maintenance vehicles, similar to the ones operated by The Downtown Partnership and its subcontractors, have been reduced to rubble.

The images show clearly where some sort of large scale force made the ramp from one floor to another collapse, nearly flattening it completely to the floor below.

These photos contribute something that appears to be net-new to the information released about this disaster.

The photos (and continuous video footage) of inside the parking garage space show very large quantity of what appear to be propane tanks of various sizes in what appears to be the areas described in the Fire Investigators report as the origination of the fire.

Video footage from within the garage verify that the of these fuel tanks were taken in the in the same location as the other photos, potentially inside the garage in the area described as inaccessible in the fire investigators report — behind a chainlink fence.

These images align with reports of what was heard on the night of the fire, as WSMV reported:

“We heard large explosions, and we didn’t know what was going on,” hotel guest Jamie Taylor said. “The whole building was filled with smoke. As soon as you walked out of your room, it just filled with smoke.”

It appears the fire started in a maintenance area the Downtown Partnership uses to store and service its streetsweepers, ATVs and other equipment, firefighters said. Most of the equipment is destroyed along with several cars that were parked nearby.

WSMV, June 10, 2025

What complicates the situation is that despite the reporting from the morning of the incident in the media, and these newly released photos and videos which appear to have been taken some time after that day (based on the conditions depicted), the fire investigation report makes no mention of these fuel tanks.

In fact, words such as “tank," “propane,” or “canister” appear nowhere in the preliminary report. The report also asserts that the area where the fire started, a storage room, was completely flattened and inaccessible for inspection. This assertion may be refuted by these images that depict the area under this ramp where the tanks are visible. Rather than being completely collapsed upon, it looks like the ramp buckled in a way where at least some of this area is still visible.

The initial fire investigators report, which is labelled as “preliminary.” details that “the fire originated… within a secure storage area beneath the ramp going up to the 5th floor… contained within the storage area was the tool room which was the area of fire origin. Examination of the tool room was impossible due to collapse. There was no available physical evidence to indicate the competent ignition source, and first fuel ignited. The investigation is closed with the classification of undetermined.”
