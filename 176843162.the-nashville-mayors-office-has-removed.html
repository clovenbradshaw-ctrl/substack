The Nashville Mayor’s Office has removed all email addresses for its employees from Nashville.gov.

This has been discovered in light of various service providers for low-income residents looking for answers as to the how exactly Nashvillian’s may qualify for free bus passes from Mayor O’Connell’s signature Choose How You Move program.

The spotty roll out of the Metro Action Commission’s provisioning of free bus passes with minimal information of what documentation is needed (taking place during a work day when most of those in desperate need for bus passes are unable to attend) has even frustrated Metro Council Members.

When an advocate attempted to reach out to the Choose How You Move’s Acting Director and Chief Program Officer at the Mayor’s Office, they found that they were newly unable to find their contact info on Nashville.gov.1

The Mayor’s Office has removed the direct emails of all of its staffers. All emails must flow through the anonymous “mayor@nashville.gov.”

In an era when federal law enforcement officials are given carte blanche to parade around our public streets in masks and the Pentagon is banning all media that is not “approved”, it is extraordinarily concerning for a “truly progressive” Mayor’s Office to eliminate any line of direct communications to its public employees.

For what it’s worth, the email addresses of all government employees in TN is definitively covered by the Tennessee Public Records Act. If it is a requirement for cough award winning cough watch dogs to submit a public records request to obtain these emails so the public can communicate with its officials appointed by their duly elected Mayor, that can be done.

But to save us all some time, the format for virtually all Nashville.gov emails is: FirstName.LastName@nashville.gov.

I do want to draw attention to the board that has alleged oversight over the implementation of Choose How You Move, the Mayor’s Advisory Committee on Transportation. The emails of those board members, many of them private citizens, are publicly listed there.

The last meeting of this committee was on Sept. 26, 2025. They might want to add some of this to the agenda for their next meeting.

When I worked for the Mayor’s Office, my boss and I had the courage to have our direct emails open for public comments on programs we rolled out. Well really, it never was even a conceivable option to not have your official government email address public.

It is personally hard for me not to attribute this change in the flow of public feedback to anything but cowardice and/or misguided attempts to control the flow of information about public projects and resources.

But, maybe it was just an IT glitch.

How the administration responds to this heightened scrutiny on their availably to speak with the public will be telling.

While removing the direct emails of employees is an IT task we can hope is beneath the Mayor himself, his new Chief of Staff, Masami Tyson, would most likely have been consulted.

On that topic, there has been surprisingly little discussion of her background in the media, and how she was previously appointed to positions by Trump acolyte Cameron Sexton, and Governor Bill Lee, and served as a senior legal official in the automotive industry.

Masami Tyson’s Relationship with Governor Bill Lee and Speaker Cameron Sexton and the Automotive Industry

Governor Bill Lee: Tyson was hired by Republican Commissioner Bob Rolfe to serve as Tennessee’s Global Director of Foreign Direct Investment and Trade (October 2018 - June 2022) during Lee’s administration. She accompanied Lee on his first economic development trip to Asia in June 2019. Lee currently serves as Honorary Co-Chair of the Japan-America Society of Tennessee alongside the Japanese Consul-General, where Tyson is Board Chair.

Nissan: Prior to her state government role, Tyson served as Senior Counsel at Nissan North America (2015-2018).

Speaker Cameron Sexton: In 2023, Republican House Speaker Cameron Sexton appointed Tyson to the Metropolitan Nashville Airport Authority Board, where she served as Secretary and Vice Chair of the Management Committee. This appointment occurred during a state restructuring effort that was subsequently overturned in court.

Sexton appointed Tyson to the Airport Board in direct contravention to Metro’s authority, yet now she sits atop the Democratic Mayor’s Office chain of command.

The ability to be open and transparent is in the hands of the administration.

I’d advise them to consider wisely how they operate next. I would tell them that: you should be careful with eliminating ways for the public to communicate with you, you need to be considerate of small d democratic norms. The ball is in your court, so act wisely when you Choose How You Move going forward.
