The wonderful Whitney Pastorek, executive producer of City Cast Nashville, was kind enough to have Kennetha Patterson—chair of the Executive Committee of the Homelessness Planning Council—and me on today’s episode of City Cast to discuss the state of homelessness in Nashville.

It was a great conversation, and the result is—objectively, I think—a really informative and engaging listen. Kennetha explained the frustrations that arise when people like her, who have “lived expertise,” are no longer paid for their contributions, while also sharing what she hopes to see more of in the future and what inspires her in this work.

I hope the Office of Homeless Services gets back to Whitney for comment, as she mentioned. A healthy dialogue about what’s working and what isn’t is the only way we’re going to navigate the next few years.

I’ve got to say, Whitney is a unique talent who can make my long-winded answers sound succinct and distill my 4,000-plus-word articles into a sentence or two—better than I ever could. I’m very grateful to have been a guest on this important local outlet to talk about what I’ve been uncovering in the homeless-services space over the past few months.
